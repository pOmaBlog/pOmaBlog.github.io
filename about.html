About pOma

The blog

*Disclaimer* This blog does not aim at providing absolute certainties (if such a thing exists). The aim is to be a conversation starter about issues faced by women in science, throughout history and today. Post will vary in content and length depending on the mood. Ranging from presenting women that have been unfairly discarded from our science book to the challenges of fair representation and bias in science and STEM.

The person behind the blog

Quite frankly, who cares? I guess the only relevant information here can be sum up like that:

Researcher in STEM. Physicist interested in the tiny building blocks of life. Clues: machine learning, lasers and statistics are all part of my daily tool box.

Rather than some ego centered BS, here comes books you can find on my bookshelves. A reading list is worth a million words (literally) after all.

Circe & Song of Achilles by Madeline Miller - Another take on famous tales, with style.
Woman on the edge of time by Marge Piercy - SciFi absolute classic.
